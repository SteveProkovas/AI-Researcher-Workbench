### üêç Python-Native & ML-Ready Datasets

These datasets integrate especially well with Python workflows (NumPy, Pandas, PyTorch, TensorFlow):

#### General / ML

* **Hugging Face Datasets** ‚Äì `datasets` library
  [https://huggingface.co/datasets](https://huggingface.co/datasets)
  Large collection of NLP, CV, audio, and multimodal datasets with streaming support.

* **TensorFlow Datasets (TFDS)**
  [https://www.tensorflow.org/datasets](https://www.tensorflow.org/datasets)
  Clean, versioned datasets with standardized splits.

* **Scikit-learn Built-in Datasets**
  [https://scikit-learn.org/stable/datasets.html](https://scikit-learn.org/stable/datasets.html)
  Classic datasets for benchmarking and prototyping.

---

#### Tabular / Real-World Data

* **Kaggle Datasets (via API)**
  [https://www.kaggle.com/datasets](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets)
  Massive real-world datasets (requires account).

* **OpenML**
  [https://www.openml.org](https://www.openml.org)
  Structured datasets with metadata, ideal for AutoML and benchmarking.

---

#### Scientific / Research

* **UCI Machine Learning Repository**
  [https://archive.ics.uci.edu](https://archive.ics.uci.edu)
  Classic research datasets used in academic papers.

* **NASA Open Data**
  [https://data.nasa.gov](https://data.nasa.gov)
  Earth observation, physics, and space data.

* **PhysioNet** (Healthcare / Signals)
  [https://physionet.org](https://physionet.org)
  Medical time-series and physiological datasets.

#### Graphs / Networks

* **Stanford SNAP Datasets**
  [https://snap.stanford.edu/data](https://snap.stanford.edu/data)

### üí° Tip

For **reproducible research and long-term projects**, prefer datasets that:

* Have versioning
* Provide metadata
* Are accessible via Python APIs

(Hugging Face Datasets + OpenML are excellent for this.)
